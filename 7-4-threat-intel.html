Tactic: Execution
Technique: Command and Scripting Interpreter
Sub-technique: PowerShell

Detection:
- Monitor PowerShell execution (Event ID 4104)
- Look for encoded commands (-enc, -e)
- Watch for download cradles (IEX, Invoke-Expression)

# Example detection query
process.name:"powershell.exe" AND 
process.command_line:(*-enc* OR *-encoded* OR *IEX* OR *downloadstring*)

âœ… Threat Intel Best Practices
  • Quality over quantity: Too many feeds create noise
  • Age matters: IOCs have a shelf life (especially IPs)
  • Context is king: Know what the IOC means
  • Automate integration: Manual IOC entry doesn't scale
  • Track effectiveness: Measure how often TI leads to detections
  • Share back: Contribute to community feeds
ğŸ’¡ Interview Tip

Question: "How do you operationalize threat intelligence?"

Answer: "I'd start by identifying relevant threat actors for our industry - financial sector faces different threats than healthcare. Then I'd select 2-3 high-quality feeds (one open source like AlienVault OTX, one commercial if budget allows). For integration, I'd configure automatic ingestion into our SIEM for IOC matching against logs, set up external dynamic lists on firewalls for blocking, and create watchlists in our EDR. For strategic intel, I'd map threat reports to MITRE ATT&CK to identify gaps in our detection coverage. I'd also track metrics - how many alerts came from TI matches, and were they true positives?"
